A situation where poor digital communication caused a serious problem can be seen in a group project scenario. In one case, a team leader shared task instructions in a class WhatsApp group using short and unclear messages without proper explanation. Some members misunderstood their responsibilities, while others assumed different deadlines. As a result, the final submission was incomplete and poorly coordinated, which affected the overall grades of the entire group. The main issue was lack of clarity, improper structure, and no confirmation of understanding among team members. This situation could have been avoided if the leader had communicated clearly using structured messages, proper language, and specific instructions. Additionally, confirming roles, setting clear deadlines, and encouraging team members to ask questions would have improved coordination. Effective digital communication is essential in academic and professional settings, as it helps avoid misunderstandings, improves teamwork, and ensures that tasks are completed accurately and on time.
